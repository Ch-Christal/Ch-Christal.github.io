      Impression,Sunrise《日出·印象》

Sunrise - Impressions is an oil painting by French Impressionist painter Claude Monet at the port of Le Havre in 1872. The painting depicts the sunrise over the harbor in the morning mist, showing the fascinating view with the wonderful variation and movement of light, and the vivid scene of light reflecting and trembling on the wide sea with the artist's light and leaping brushstrokes. Now in the Musée des Beaux-Arts de Marmottan, Paris.

This work breaks away from the traditional limitations of subject matter and composition and focuses on the changing appearance of the atmosphere of light, entirely based on the perception of visual experience. It is one of the most typical of Monet's paintings and the most reputable of his later classics, and is the founding work of the Impressionist School.

Oscar Claude Monet. He is one of the people who played a fundamental role in the emergence of Impressionism. Some experts consider him to be one of the most important painters of the Impressionist movement. During his lifetime, Monet produced about two and a half thousand paintings and drawings.

Claude Monet's life was difficult. He was very poor. Only at the end of the 19th century did his works become so successful that he could sell them. Nowadays, people can see his paintings in museums all over the world.






Édouard Manet

Name: Edouard Manet
Born: January 23, 1832; Paris, France

A pivotal figure in the transition from Realism to Impressionism, Edouard Manet was an influential painter who left his own unique mark on the art world. As a child, his father was a judge, who wanted him to pursue a career in law, but his uncle, recognizing little Edouard’s talents, encouraged his interest in art, often taking him the Louvre. It was not until Manet failed two entrance exams into the French Navy that his father relented to his son’s wishes and allowed him to start art lessons under the academic painter Thomas Couture, who had him copy the works of the great masters in the Louvre. The young artist was also influenced by Frans Hals, Diego Velazquez, and Francisco Goya.
